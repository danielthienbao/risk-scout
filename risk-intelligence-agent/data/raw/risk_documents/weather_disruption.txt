Storm systems, monsoon surges, and extreme weather events can sharply increase operational uncertainty on ocean and inland routes. Heavy rain, high winds, and flooding reduce throughput at ports, close terminals, and force rerouting that lengthens transit time. Seasonal weather variability can also affect first-mile and last-mile trucking reliability, especially in flood-prone corridors. Organizations should include weather-derived risk multipliers in ETA forecasts and pre-plan alternate routes to reduce service-level failures during extreme weather disruptions.
