Supplier insolvency and bankruptcy events create direct fulfillment risk by interrupting production, delaying purchase order acceptance, and reducing quality consistency during financial distress. Warning signals include shrinking credit terms, abrupt workforce reductions, rising defect rates, and delayed responses to replenishment requests. If a critical supplier files for protection, downstream buyers may face component shortages and expedited procurement costs. A resilient risk strategy includes financial health monitoring, dual sourcing, and contingency inventory buffers for vulnerable supplier tiers.
